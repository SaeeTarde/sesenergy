The Energy Audit Process

A rigorous, systematic methodology — from first walk-through to final implementation.

How We Work

A Structured 3-Phase Audit Methodology

Our detailed energy audit follows the methodology prescribed by the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE), Government of India — adapted and refined through 20+ years of field experience.

📋 Phase 1 — Pre-Audit
1

Plan & Organize

Informal walk-through with Energy Manager and Production/Plant Manager. Resource planning and audit team formation.

  • Organize audit instruments and time frame
  • Macro data collection (suitable to industry type)
  • Familiarization with plant process and activities
  • First-hand observation of current operations and practices
2

Awareness & Orientation Meeting

A 2–3 hour awareness program is conducted with all divisional heads and relevant personnel to build cooperation and establish communication channels.

  • Issue questionnaires for each department
  • Orientation and awareness creation
  • Establish energy conservation champions in each department
🔍 Phase 2 — Audit Phase
3

Primary Data Collection

Systematic gathering of all energy and process data — the foundation of the entire audit.

  • Process Flow Diagram preparation
  • Energy and Utility Diagram mapping
  • Historical data analysis and baseline data collection
  • Single Line power distribution diagram
  • Water, compressed air, and steam distribution mapping
  • Annual energy bills and consumption pattern analysis
  • Design and operating data review (manuals, log sheets, name plates)
4

Survey & Monitoring with Instruments

On-site measurements using calibrated instruments to collect accurate, real-time data.

  • Motor survey and load analysis
  • Measure process equipment Energy efficiency assessment(boilers, electrical melting furnaces, induction billet heaters, thermic fluid heaters, DG sets, refrigeration systems, compressed air systems, pumps, fans, lighting)
  • Insulation survey
  • Lighting survey (lux levels)
  • Compressed air leak detection
  • Boiler and furnace flue gas analysis
  • Thermal imaging survey
  • Comparison of operating data vs. design specifications
5

Detailed Trials & Experiments

For identified energy guzzlers, SES conducts detailed performance trials to quantify losses and savings potential.

  • 24-hour power monitoring (MD, PF, kWh trends)
  • Load variation studies for pumps, fans, and compressors
  • Boiler and furnace efficiency trials
  • Equipment performance experiments
  • Refrigeration and chilling system performance
6

Energy Use Analysis

Comprehensive energy and material balance to pinpoint every source of waste in the plant.

  • Energy and material balance for all processes
  • Energy loss and waste analysis
  • Water balance for the entire plant
  • Specific energy consumption calculation
7

Identify Energy Conservation (ENCON) Opportunities

Drawing on data analysis, field observations, and technology knowledge to develop a comprehensive list of conservation measures.

  • Identification and consolidation of ENCON measures
  • Brain-storming and value analysis sessions
  • Review of ideas suggested by plant personnel
  • Vendor consultation for newer, efficient technologies
8

Cost-Benefit Analysis & Prioritization

Every recommendation is assessed for technical feasibility, economic viability, and implementation priority.

  • Classification into no-cost, low-cost, medium-cost, and high-cost measures
  • Payback period calculation for each measure
  • Prioritization by short-term, medium-term, and long-term actions
  • Selection of most promising projects for immediate action
🚀 Phase 3 — Implementation & Follow-Up
9

Report Preparation & Management Presentation

A comprehensive audit report is prepared and formally presented to top management — making the case for investment and action.

  • Detailed documentation of all findings
  • Presentation to top management with financial justification
  • Action plan with schedule and responsibility assignment
10

Implementation Assistance & Monitoring

SES and its group companies provide implementation support to ensure recommendations are actually executed — and savings are realized.

  • Assistance with implementation of ENCON recommendations
  • Performance monitoring post-implementation
  • Periodic review and progress tracking
  • Measurement and verification of actual savings achieved
