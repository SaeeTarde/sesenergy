Energy & Utility Audits

The foundation of every successful energy conservation programme

An energy audit is the key to a systematic approach for decision-making in energy management. As defined under the Energy Conservation Act 2001, it involves verification, monitoring, and analysis of energy use, followed by a technical report with cost-benefit analysis and an actionable implementation plan.

SES conducts three types of energy audits depending on your plant's needs and the depth of analysis required:

🔎 Preliminary Audit

Uses existing data to quickly establish energy consumption baselines, estimate savings potential, and identify immediate no-cost/low-cost improvements. Ideal as a first step.

🎯 Targeted Energy Audit

Focuses on specific systems — lighting, compressed air, boilers, or HVAC — with detailed analysis of energy flows and costs for the targeted area.

📋 Detailed Energy Audit

A comprehensive, multi-phase study covering pre-audit planning, primary data collection, 24-hour monitoring, energy and material balances, and a complete ENCON recommendations report presented to top management.
